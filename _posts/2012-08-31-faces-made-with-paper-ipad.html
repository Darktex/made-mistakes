Have you heard of Paper by 53 for iPad — an application optimized for sketching, drawing, writing, and creating ideas? I’d encourage you to download it for free from the App Store and try out the fountain pen tool. If your experience is anything like mine, you’ll quickly drop a few bucks to purchase the remaining tools.

I’ve been drawing and “painting” with Paper by 53 nonstop for months now. When I’m not drawing I actively search the #MadeWithPaper tag on both Twitter and Tumblr for inspiration. The creativity and skill of everyone posting with this tag is amazing and has influenced me to start my own project.

Project #PaperFaces Explained

I have this goal of drawing at least one portrait a day using an iPad, stylus, and Paper by 53 — for as long as I can. Retweet the message below if you’d like to volunteer and I’ll add you to the list. Using your profile photo (ideally it’s large so I can see a ton of detail) as reference, I’ll sketch my interpretation of it and @reply you with the finished drawing.

What’s the Point of Drawing on an iPad?

Paper by 53 and the iPad have reconnected art and working with my hands in a way I never thought possible. The last time I drew or painted on a regular basis — was before I started working full time as a graphic designer in 2001. Paper by 53 in conjunction with a stylus, is close to how I work with pen and ink washes. Replicating the feel of these traditional drawing tools, in a portable and mess free package. Not to mention, it adds a useful feature that lets you travel into the past by rewinding a mistake after it happens.

There is a sense of focus and clarity found in each illustration I create with Paper by 53. Compositions harmonize because the limited color palette and tools compliment each other so well. Other drawing and painting apps might have greater flexibility, but they tend to overwhelm with all their features and options. This smaller toolset has led me to a deeper understanding of how each works and hopefully a greater level of mastering “the art of seeing.”
