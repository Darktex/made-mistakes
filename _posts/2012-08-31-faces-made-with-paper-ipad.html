So there’s this iPad app, Paper by FiftyThree — an application optimized for sketching, drawing, writing, and creating ideas. Download it for free from the App Store and try out the fountain pen tool if you haven’t already. Hands down this is my favorite and most used app since purchasing the new iPad.

I’ve been drawing and “painting” with Paper by FiftyThree nonstop for months now. When I’m not drawing, I actively search the #MadeWithPaper tag on both Twitter and Tumblr for inspiration. The creativity and skill of everyone posting is amazing and has inspired me to start my own project.

#PaperFaces Project Explained

The idea of the PaperFaces Project is to draw at least one portrait a day for the next year, using iPad and the Paper by FiftyThree app. Retweet the message below if you’d like to volunteer and I’ll add you to the list. Using your profile photo (ideally nice and large so I can see a ton of detail) as reference, I’ll sketch my interpretation of it and @reply you with the finished drawing.

Retweet and I'll draw your profile picture using Paper on my iPad #MadeWithPaper twitter.com/mmistakes/stat…

— Michael Rose (@mmistakes) August 29, 2012

Update: Wow! The amount of volunteers is over 500 1,650 now. I’m commited to drawing 730 profile pictures — that’s two a day for the next year. If I’m not burned out after that, maybe I’ll get to all the other faces…

With the insane amount of interest, I decided to include a way to guarantee I draw your portrait – donate $5 USD if you’re cool and up to $50 if you’re feeling awesomely generous. Just hit the PayPal button below and include your Twitter username and email in the ordering comments so I can send you a high resolution drawing. If you’d like me to use a different photograph of your face, just include a link to it in the PayPal notes.

PS. I’m jumping anyone who donates to the project to the top of the list. So if you’re the impatient type and want a portrait in the next day or two, that’s your best option. With the increased demand this month, and it being the holidays and all, there might be a few extra days of delay tacked on…

Please note. This is for a digital version of the drawing only. You will not receive a print snail mailed to you. Although the 2048×1536 .jpg sent should look nice as a framed 8×10" if you decide to print it yourself…

Upon completion I will post each drawing to the #PaperFaces Portrait Gallery — so bookmark away to keep up with the project. Alternately, you can follow me on Twitter or search for #PaperFaces on Twitter and Tumblr.

What’s the Point of Drawing on iPad?

Paper by FiftyThree and iPad have reconnected art and working with my hands in a way I never thought possible. The last time I drew or painted on a regular basis — was before I started working full time as a graphic designer in 2001. Paper by 53 in conjunction with a stylus, is close to how I work with pen and ink washes. Replicating the feel of these traditional drawing tools, in a portable and mess free package. Not to mention, it adds a useful feature that lets you travel into the past by rewinding a mistake after it happens.

There is a sense of focus and clarity found in each illustration I create with Paper by FiftyThree. Compositions harmonize because the limited color palette and tools compliment each other so well. Other drawing and painting apps might have greater flexibility, but they tend to overwhelm with all their features and options. This smaller toolset has led me to a deeper understanding of how each works and hopefully a greater level of mastering “the art of seeing.”
