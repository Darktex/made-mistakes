Just saw that TealPoint Software released a WebOS skin (launcher) for Palm devices like the Centro and Treos called TealOS. They have a 30 day trial version you can download and give before purchasing a full version for $14.95.

Took a few seconds after first running the TealOS launcher for it to create thumbnail and icon images of all my applications, but after that I was presented with a home screen that looks like the webOS UI that’s going to debut on the Palm Pre later this year. You can navigate through open applications with the new card view—remove them with a flick of your finger, change their order, etc. It’s a little sluggish when switching between cards and they don’t have live previews, but they do accurately represent each of the applications and have some nice transitions and transparency to them.

At the bottom of the home screen you’ll see a Quick Launcher bar that can be customized. Just like Palm Pre, sliding your finger from the bottom of the screen upwards opens the Wave Laucher, which lets you open applications when not on the home screen. The Wave Launcher can also be customized with whatever application icons you see fit.

I’ve never been one to purchase launchers for my Palm devices, but this one looks polished enough if you’re into that sort of thing. If you want to give your aging smartphone an update—I suggest downloading the trial version of TealOS to hold you over until the Palm Pre releases.
