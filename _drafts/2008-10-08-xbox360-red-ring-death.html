About a month ago my Xbox 360 finally died and began flashing the dreaded “Red Ring of Death“ at startup. For some time now my Xbox 360 exhibited signs of dying—as it would lock up frequently and give me disc read errors on brand new games. Seeing how pretty much everyone I know has had problems with their Xbox 360’s, I was really surprised mine lasted this long.

First thing I did after getting the RRoD was to check Microsoft’s website to confirm that my Xbox 360 was indeed dead. After determining that it was time to send it back to Microsoft, I hit up their support pages to start the process.

I’ll give it to Microsoft on this one. Registering my Xbox 360 and requesting a repair order was extremely easy and painless. Took me a few seconds to fill out the necessary online forms and check my warranty info (good thing they extended the date). Best part was I never had to call Microsoft and try to explain my problem to some horrible customer service representative.

What followed next was the hard part…waiting patiently for Microsoft to mail me a box to pack my Xbox 360 into and ship it back to them. After 3 days it arrived and included clear instructions (with pictures) describing how to safely pack it up. Took the box to local UPS Store and had them ship it out, all on Microsoft’s dime.

About two weeks later I received a phone call from UPS notifying me of the estimated deliver date of my Xbox 360 and that someone needed to be present and sign for it. I knew the date prior to the phone call since I made a routine of checking my Xbox 360’s repair status from xbox.com everyday. But still, it was nice to know that they were making an effort to keep me well informed.

I’m not sure if they swapped out the bad hardware or just gave me a refurbished console. I guess I should have written down my console’s serial number prior to shipping, so I could have compared the two. Either way, I got my Xbox 360 back and it’s had zero problems since. The disc read errors I had previously are gone, which leads me to believe they gave me a different console. Unless of course they replaced the DVD drive as well, which I doubt they did. It even seems to run quieter which is a nice treat.

No complaints from me on this whole process. I just hope I don’t have to go through it again if this unit dies…
