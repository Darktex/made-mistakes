Saturday afternoon I went to my mother’s house to pick up some old baseball cards and a few board games they found in the attic. After filling my lungs with ten year old attic dust I sat in the back room by the fire and rested. My mom had saved the Gusto from Friday’s Buffalo News and wanted to show me an article about Sal’s.

Sal’s is this small bar in Depew, NY situated between a few houses in the middle of suburbia. I can remember the first time I went there and thinking it was the strangest place for a bar. I thought I was walking into someone’s house and would have never noticed the place since it’s on some side street in what looks like every other average Buffalo suburb.

I was promised the best wings in Buffalo. I won’t go as far as dethroning that title from Duff’s Famous Wings on Sheridan Drive, but their wings are a lot more crispy and come smaller (which I like), but have more of a barbecue sauce flavor mixed in. I’ve always gotten medium hot because my brother assured me that hot would melt my face. Well last time we went I thought “Shit! I can do Duff’s hot… (where medium is hot and hot is very, very hot!) I can surely do Sal’s hot.” WRONG! I ate the first five no problem and by number six I was in pain. Not even the pitcher of Blue could cool me down.

The wings were the draw for our party of four, eager to sample the much-heralded breading and medium hot and barbecue flavors. It was a one-woman operation as Debbie poured drafts at the well-stocked bar and cooked food orders from Sal’s modest menu (the Cajun fries aren’t listed, but ask for them – they’re a necessary complement to any wing order). A quick look in Sal’s postage-stamp sized but immaculate kitchen suggests anything but the basics would be too ambitious.
