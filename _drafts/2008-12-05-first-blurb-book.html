The other day I finally got around to downloading Blurb’s BookSmart™ software to layout my book. In the future I really want to create an art book using all my journal drawings, writing, and collages and then get it printed with a hardcover. So to test out the service and see how good the print quality is I created a simple book using photography my girlfriend Wendy and myself took over the past six months.

Using the BookSmart™ software is super easy. You can link up to online websites (ie: Flickr and Picasa) that might be hosting your photos for easy importing or you can point it to a folder on your hard-drive. Once your photos are imported you can start dropping in photos, giving them captions, adding blocks of copy, and much more. There are a few themes to choose from and numerous templates to get that perfect page layout.

After proofing the pages on screen and making sure everything looked how I wanted, I uploaded my book to Blurb’s site. From here I was able to order copies and make it available to the public to view and purchase. I think this is great if you want to create books and sell them on the cheap since you don’t have to pay a fortune for a large print run. Print on demand is great!

You can preview the first couple of pages from my book and check out a ton of other great books for inspiration.

I’ll follow up with a future post once I get the book and analyze the quality. With the holidays in full bloom it might take an extra week to print and ship. I sure hope it arrives in time for Christmas…
